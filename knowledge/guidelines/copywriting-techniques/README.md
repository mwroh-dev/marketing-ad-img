# Global Copywriting Techniques

Brand-agnostic principles. No brand, product, or category specifics belong here.

---

## Hooks

A hook must stop the scroll in the first 1–2 lines. Types:
- **Question hook:** Surfaces a latent pain or desire. Risk: if the answer is "no," you lose them instantly.
- **Contradiction hook:** Opens with something surprising or counter-intuitive.
- **Outcome hook:** Leads with the result, not the product.
- **Empathy hook:** Names the pain in the reader's own language before offering anything.

**Rule:** The hook must speak to the audience's current mental state, not the marketer's goal.

---

## Objection Handling

Surface the objection before the reader can raise it silently, then neutralize it.
- Name the objection explicitly ("You might wonder…").
- Provide evidence, not reassurance (testimony, spec, demo > "trust us").
- One objection per creative unit — addressing multiple implies doubt.

---

## Conversational Patterns

- Write at the reading level of the target audience, not the brand's internal voice.
- Short sentences lower cognitive load.
- Use second-person ("you") to collapse distance.
- Avoid industry jargon unless the audience self-identifies with it.

---

## Claim Caution

- Every claim needs a backing source before it enters a creative.
- Superlatives ("best", "fastest", "only") require proof or must be scoped ("best in [defined set]").
- Implicit claims (images showing outcomes) are subject to the same standard as explicit ones.
- When evidence is thin, use hedged language ("most customers find…") rather than absolute claims.
