# Global Layout Principles

Brand-agnostic principles. No brand, product, or category specifics belong here.

---

## Visual Hierarchy

Viewers scan, they do not read. Design for the scan order:
1. **Focal point** — one dominant element that captures attention first (hero image or headline).
2. **Supporting element** — secondary information that earns the next second of attention.
3. **Call to action** — appears after the viewer is primed, not before.

Rule: if everything competes for first attention, nothing wins.

---

## Grid

Use an underlying grid to create alignment and breathing room.
- Consistent margins signal intentionality.
- Asymmetric layouts create energy; symmetric layouts create stability.
- Choose the tension appropriate to the brand's emotional register.

---

## Focal Point

- One image or headline should dominate. Limit primary focal points to one per frame.
- Product should be placed so the natural eye path leads into it, not away.
- Negative space is not wasted space — it directs attention.

---

## Text Density

- Mobile-first: if text requires zooming, it will not be read.
- Maximum 2–3 lines of body copy visible without scrolling in feed formats.
- Every word that does not earn its place weakens the ones that do.
- Hierarchy: headline > sub-headline > body > legal/fine print. Never invert.
