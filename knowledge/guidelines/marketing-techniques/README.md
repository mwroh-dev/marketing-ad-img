# Global Marketing Techniques

Brand-agnostic principles. No brand, product, or category specifics belong here.

---

## Frameworks

### AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
Sequential persuasion arc. Use for full-funnel creatives where the viewer has no prior awareness.
**When NOT to use:** Retargeting — viewer already has awareness; skip straight to Desire or Action.

### PAS (Problem → Agitation → Solution)
Lead with a pain point, intensify it, then present relief. Effective for cold audiences with a clear pain.
**When NOT to use:** When the audience is not aware they have the problem; start with Awareness moves instead.

### BAB (Before → After → Bridge)
Show transformation. Effective when the outcome is vivid and believable.
**When NOT to use:** When the "after" state requires a long proof chain the ad cannot supply.

### JTBD (Jobs To Be Done)
Frame the product as hired to complete a functional, social, or emotional job.
**When NOT to use:** Pure impulse or novelty purchases where no clear job exists.

---

## Influence Levers

### Social Proof
Signals that others have already chosen this. Reduces perceived risk.
**Caution:** Must be specific (numbers, verbatim quotes) to be credible; vague social proof backfires.

### Scarcity / Urgency
Limited stock or time creates loss-aversion pressure.
**Caution:** Fake scarcity destroys trust on repeated exposure. Only use when real.

### Contrast
Highlight the gap between before and after, or this product versus the default alternative.
**When to use:** Whenever a viewer is likely comparing options or stuck with a frustrating status quo.

### Specificity
Concrete numbers ("3.5L", "12 minutes") outperform vague claims ("large", "fast").
**Rule:** Replace adjectives with measurements wherever possible.
